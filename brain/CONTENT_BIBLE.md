## CC's Content Bible (2026-03-07)

### 3 Daily Pillars (minimum 3 posts/day)

| Pillar | Frequency | Format | Purpose |
|--------|-----------|--------|---------|
| **60-Day Sobriety Log** | 1x daily | 30-90sec talking head | Accountability + transformation arc. People follow journeys. |
| **Daily Quote Drop** | 1x daily | 5-15sec, face + text overlay | Low effort, high reach. Quote does the work, face = credibility. |
| **22-Year-Old CEO Log** | 1x daily | 30-60sec, one decision/win/lesson | Building in public = magnetic. Raw and direct. |

### Hook Bank (4 Categories)
- **Pattern Interrupt**: "Stop scrolling", "I wasn't gonna post this but...", "Nobody talks about this"
- **Curiosity Gap**: "The thing they don't tell you about [X]...", "Most people do this wrong"
- **Credibility/Proof**: "[X] days in -- here's exactly what changed", "I built this at 22 with no funding"
- **Relatability/Emotion**: "If you're going through [X], this is for you", "Nobody clapped when I started"

### 7 Pacing Rules
1. Hook in 0-2 seconds (first sentence IS the video)
2. One idea per video (specificity > breadth)
3. Cut ALL filler words in edit (jump cuts are fine)
4. Speak to one person (camera = that one person)
5. End with a punch, not a fade (hard cut after final sentence)
6. 30-90 second sweet spot
7. Film 3-5, post the best 3

### Pre-Film Checklist
- Hook written out before filming
- One clear point identified
- Camera at eye level
- Good lighting (face lit, no harsh shadows)
- First word = hook. No intro. No "hey guys."

## Jeremy Miner / NEPQ Framework (2026-03-07)

### Core Philosophy
Traditional selling triggers resistance. NEPQ (Neuro-Emotional Persuasion Questions) disarms it by making the prospect persuade themselves. Three communication tiers:
1. **Telling** (least persuasive) -- pushing, pitching
2. **Discussion** (moderate) -- consultative, logic-based
3. **Dialogue** (most persuasive) -- questions that cause self-persuasion

### NEPQ Question Sequence (for OASIS calls/emails)

| Stage | Type | Purpose | OASIS Example |
|-------|------|---------|---------------|
| 1 | Connecting | Build trust, shift from "sales call" | "Can you walk me through how you handle lead follow-up?" |
| 2 | Situation | Uncover surface facts + hidden friction | "What happens when a new inquiry comes in after hours?" |
| 3 | Problem Awareness | Get THEM to articulate pain | "What happens when that process breaks down?" |
| 4 | Solution Awareness | What they've tried (and what failed) | "What have you tried to speed up response times?" |
| 5 | Consequence | Visualize cost of inaction | "What happens if this stays the same for 6-12 months?" |
| 6 | Commitment | Secure next step without pressure | "Based on what we've discussed, worth exploring further?" |

### 5 Tones of Voice
1. **Curious** -- genuine interest when asking questions
2. **Confused** -- strategically signals "I don't understand" to get them to expand
3. **Concerned** -- empathy when they reveal a problem
4. **Challenging** -- sparingly, for urgency
5. **Playful/Relaxed** -- openers and pattern interrupts to disarm

### Pattern Interrupt Principles
- Break the "sales pitch -> delete" brain loop
- Must be: unexpected, self-aware, honest, curiosity-driven
- Pattern interrupts have a shelf life -- rotate regularly
- DON'T use after 4+ follow-ups (feels desperate at that point)

### Cold Email Openers (OASIS-adapted)

**Subject lines:**
- "This is awkward, but..."
- "Don't open this email"
- "Quick question about [Business Name]"
- "[First name], honest question"

**Opening lines:**
1. **Self-aware**: "I'll be honest -- this is a cold email. If it's not relevant, you'll know in 8 seconds..."
2. **Curiosity cliffhanger**: "We found a fix that cut a clinic's missed-call rate by 40%. Curious if same problem applies?"
3. **Confused/uncertain (Miner signature)**: "I'm not sure if this would even be relevant for [Business], but..."
4. **Consequence question**: "When someone calls [Business] after 5pm Friday, what happens to that lead?"
5. **Role reversal**: "We're looking for 2-3 [service] businesses in [area] to test something. Not sure if you'd be the right fit."
6. **Honest time-thief**: "This email will cost you 12 seconds. Here's the deal: [value prop]. Worth a 15-min call?"

### Anti-Patterns (NEVER DO)
- "I'm reaching out because..." (instant delete)
- Leading with features or company credentials
- Sounding overly enthusiastic/certain (triggers resistance)
- "Do you have 15 minutes?" (answer is always no)
- "Just checking in" / "circling back" (signals nothing new)
- Pitching before understanding their situation

### Key Rules for Written Outreach
- Never lead with product -- lead with THEIR problem
- Use "I'm not sure if..." framing (detachment kills resistance)
- Ask, don't tell ("How are you handling X?" not "We automate X")
- Consequence questions > urgency tactics
- Tonality in writing = word choice: curious ("I was wondering"), confused ("I'm not sure if"), concerned ("I noticed that")
