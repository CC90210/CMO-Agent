# MARKETING CANON — The Frameworks Maven Grounds Every Decision In

> Every Maven recommendation, ad draft, campaign plan, and content angle should trace to at least one of these canonical frameworks. If a decision can't be defended with one of these, it's probably fashion, not strategy.

This doc is Maven's source library. Skills reference it by pointer: `per brand voice — see MARKETING_CANON.md § April Dunford`.

---

## The 10 Pillars (memorize)

### 1. **April Dunford — Positioning is the foundation**
- Book: *Obviously Awesome* (2019) + *Sales Pitch* (2023)
- Core framework: positioning = competitive alternatives + unique attributes + value → for a specific best-fit customer
- The 5 inputs: (1) alternatives customers compare you to, (2) your unique attributes, (3) the value those deliver, (4) the characteristics of customers who care, (5) the market category you anchor in
- Apply: every new brand Maven manages (OASIS, PropFlow, SunBiz, Nostalgic) gets a positioning doc BEFORE any copy is written
- Anti-pattern: writing ads before the positioning is locked. Every "our copy isn't converting" problem is usually a positioning problem.

### 2. **Byron Sharp — How Brands Grow (Ehrenberg-Bass)**
- Book: *How Brands Grow* (2010) + *How Brands Grow Part 2* (2015)
- Core laws: brands grow by acquiring LIGHT buyers (not deepening heavy-buyer loyalty). Mental availability × physical availability = growth.
- **Double Jeopardy Law**: small brands have both fewer buyers AND lower loyalty — can't out-loyalty your way to growth.
- **Duplication of Purchase Law**: your customers share their wallet with competitors in proportion to market share, not preference.
- Apply: Maven should NEVER over-index on retention tactics at sub-scale — acquisition > retention until product-market fit
- Metric focus: **reach** (unique people exposed) before **frequency** (exposures per person)

### 3. **Mark Ritson — Strategy before tactics, brand before activation**
- Source: MarketingWeek columns, Mini MBA in Marketing
- Mantra: *"Diagnose before prescribe."* Research → strategy → tactics. In that order. Never the reverse.
- Brand codes (Jennifer Aaker's dimensions): sincerity, excitement, competence, sophistication, ruggedness. Pick your slot, be consistent.
- Apply: before launching any campaign, Maven answers: (1) who's the target segment? (2) what's the positioning? (3) what's the objective — rational or emotional? (4) what are the brand codes? (5) only then: channel + creative + budget
- Anti-pattern: "let's test Instagram Reels" as an opening strategy move

### 4. **April Dunford (again) — Sales Pitch structure**
- 4-part pitch: (1) Insight about the market, (2) Alternatives and their flaws, (3) Your differentiated approach, (4) Payoff
- Apply: every sales page + every long-form pitch Maven writes for OASIS/PropFlow follows this. No "bio first, product second" — start with the market insight.

### 5. **Alex Hormozi — Offers + Leads**
- Books: *$100M Offers* (2021), *$100M Leads* (2023)
- **Value Equation**: (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) / (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice). Maximize numerator, minimize denominator.
- **Lead flow frames**: 4 ways to get leads — warm outreach, post content, paid ads, cold outreach. Every brand needs multiple. Starts with one until $1M.
- **The Grand Slam Offer**: stack bonuses, remove risk, make it a no-brainer. Scarcity + urgency + guarantee.
- Apply: every OASIS offer Maven drafts goes through the Value Equation check; every lead-gen campaign picks ONE of the 4 frames + compounds

### 6. **Russell Brunson — Funnel architecture**
- Books: *DotCom Secrets* (2015), *Expert Secrets* (2017), *Traffic Secrets* (2020)
- Funnel types: survey funnel, webinar funnel, high-ticket, tripwire, book funnel, application funnel
- **Hook → Story → Offer** sequence on every page
- **Dream 100**: identify the 100 people who already have your ideal customers, go through them, not around them
- Apply: when Maven designs a funnel for a coaching client or info-product, it picks the right funnel TYPE first, not the copy first. Structure > sentences.

### 7. **Jeremy Miner — NEPQ sales methodology**
- NEPQ = Neuro-Emotional Persuasion Questioning
- Question flow: Connection → Situation → Problem Awareness → Solution Awareness → Consequence → Qualifying → Transition → Presentation → Commitment
- Anti-pattern: "push" selling. NEPQ uses neutral tonality + neutral language + objection-inviting reframes
- Apply: every PULSE doctrine objection handler + every sales-call script Maven drafts is NEPQ-structured. Also every DM conversation Maven writes.

### 8. **Chet Holmes — The Ultimate Sales Machine**
- Book: *The Ultimate Sales Machine* (2007)
- **Dream 100**: same as Brunson's adaptation — identify the tier-1 targets and stack touches over time
- **Buyer pyramid**: 3% actively looking, 7% open to looking, 30% not thinking, 30% don't think they're interested, 30% know they're not. Most content goes after the 3%; the win is content for the 60% "not thinking" crowd with education-first hooks.
- Apply: Maven's content calendar must include at least 60% "educational/not-pitching" content targeting the non-shopping majority

### 9. **Seth Godin — Permission + Tribe**
- Books: *Permission Marketing* (1999), *Tribes* (2008), *This Is Marketing* (2018)
- Core: marketing is the act of MAKING CHANGE for a specific group of people you want to serve. Not interruption; invitation.
- **Smallest Viable Audience**: serve 1,000 true fans before trying to serve a million
- Apply: Maven prioritizes depth-of-resonance (does this speak to OUR ICP?) over breadth-of-reach (how many saw it?)

### 10. **Rory Sutherland — Behavioral economics in marketing**
- Book: *Alchemy* (2019); also Ogilvy Vice Chair
- **The psycho-logic insight**: humans optimize for feelings, not utility. Price increases can increase demand (Veblen goods). Narrative wraps drive more than features.
- **Signalling > functionality**: people don't buy the thing; they buy what owning the thing says about them.
- Apply: when Maven drafts OASIS positioning, it asks "what does it SIGNAL to hire OASIS?" — not just "what does it DO?"

## Anti-Canon (the bad advice to actively reject)

### "Just post more content"
Volume without strategy is noise. Apply: Ritson's diagnosis-before-prescription.

### "Your competitors are using X, you should too"
Fashion, not strategy. Apply: Dunford's unique attributes test — do you have a defensible reason to play the same game?

### "Your customer retention is your biggest growth lever"
Almost never true below $1M ARR. Apply: Sharp's Double Jeopardy — light buyers are the growth driver, not loyalty.

### "People love the product so we don't need marketing"
Mental availability decays weekly. Apply: Sharp's mental-availability primacy.

### "Nobody reads long copy anymore"
High-intent buyers read 2,000+ words before buying expensive things. Apply: Brunson's page architectures, Hormozi's stacked-offer long-form.

### "Just use AI to write everything"
Reduces signal, increases noise, kills brand voice. Apply: Maven uses AI for DRAFTS, CC edits for voice. No AI auto-publishing without human voice pass.

## How Maven Uses This Canon Operationally

1. **Before any campaign**: read the 10 Pillars section again. Ask: which 2-3 apply most directly?
2. **Every sales page draft**: Dunford (positioning) + Brunson (funnel) + Hormozi (offer) are mandatory inputs.
3. **Every content piece**: Sharp (mental availability) + Holmes (buyer pyramid) + Godin (smallest viable audience) are mandatory inputs.
4. **Every objection handler**: Miner's NEPQ structure.
5. **Every audience research cycle**: Fitzpatrick's Mom Test for interviews + Sharp's category-membership research.
6. **Every pricing decision**: Blair Enns + Hormozi Value Equation + Sutherland signalling check.

## How to Add To This Canon

New frameworks earn a spot when they:
1. Have proven across multiple verticals (not just one)
2. Are named by one of Maven's existing canonical voices (e.g., April Dunford mentions someone → worth investigating)
3. Produce repeatable results when applied to Maven's own campaigns
4. Resist fashion — something still cited after 5 years

Propose additions in `memory/PATTERNS.md` with evidence; promote after 3 successful applications.
