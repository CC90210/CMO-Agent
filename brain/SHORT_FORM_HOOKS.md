# Short-Form Hook System

Use this for CC's personal short-form scripts, Reels, TikToks, Shorts, and first lines on X/LinkedIn when the post needs short-form gravity.

## Core Principle

The hook should create recognition, not ask for sympathy.

Make the viewer feel "wait, I have seen that" or "I need the next sentence." Do not center weak circumstances as the emotional pitch. Mention rejection, no-shows, or ugly founder days only when they become proof for a sharper idea.

## Locked CC-Provided Starters

These hooks are short-form patterns, not full scripts. Pick one pattern before drafting and name it in the output.

1. **Nobody mentions this.**
   - Function: hidden cost, neglected truth, quiet problem.
   - Example: "Nobody mentions this about AI tools: the prompt is rarely the bottleneck."

2. **I wish I knew this earlier.**
   - Function: hindsight, earned lesson, avoidable mistake.
   - Example: "I wish I knew this earlier: a messy sales day can still produce momentum."

3. **Pause for a second.**
   - Function: command attention before a reframe.
   - Example: "Pause for a second. The lead you lost probably did not need more convincing; they needed faster follow-up."

4. **Ever notice this pattern?**
   - Function: pattern recognition, repeated behavior, market observation.
   - Example: "Ever notice this pattern? The businesses with the simplest systems usually respond the fastest."

5. **Here's a real truth:**
   - Function: blunt principle, founder law, no-fluff claim.
   - Example: "Here's a real truth: prompts do not compound. Systems do."

## Expansion Rule

CC referenced a 10-hook set; only the five above were visible in this session. Treat these five as locked and leave room for five more when supplied. Do not invent the missing five and pretend they came from CC.

## Transform Rules

- Given a topic, generate 5-10 hook candidates by rotating these patterns.
- Keep hooks concrete; include the noun, setting, behavior, or market whenever possible.
- Avoid "nobody showed up..." as the default opening because it can read needy. Reframe toward the asset, rep, system, market, or founder lesson.
- For founder content, prefer calm observational hooks over wounded hooks.
- The hook is not the whole take. Sentence two must pay it off immediately.
- First sentence must be short enough to say in one breath.

## Quality Gate

- Could CC say this with certainty, not apology?
- Does it create curiosity without vague hype?
- Does it imply a specific story or useful principle?
- Is the hook under 12 words when possible?
- Does it avoid hustle-bro moralizing?

## Use Cases

- Personal founder videos
- AI Oracle explainers
- CEO Log/building-in-public videos
- Local-business/software lessons
- X/LinkedIn first lines when short-form structure fits
