## The 4 Pre-Flight Checks (run before any copy is written)

1. **Positioning locked?** — per [[MARKETING_CANON]] § April Dunford. If not, go write positioning first. Skip this and every downstream word is guesswork.
2. **Brand voice loaded?** — read the correct [[oasis-ai|OASIS]] / [[conaugh-personal|Conaugh]] / [[propflow|PropFlow]] / [[nostalgic-requests|Nostalgic]] / [[sunbiz-funding|SunBiz]] voice block.
3. **Audience segment named?** — Sharp's light-buyer vs heavy-buyer, Holmes' buyer pyramid tier (3% / 7% / 30% / 30% / 30%). Writing "for everyone" means writing for no one ([[MARKETING_CANON]] § Godin).
4. **Canon citation noted?** — at least 1 pillar. If you can't cite, you're not yet recommending — you're drafting.

## Hook Formulas (Canon-Derived)

### For top-of-funnel reach ([[MARKETING_CANON]] § Sharp, § Holmes)
Targets the 30% "not thinking about this" — NOT the 3% actively shopping.

- **Pattern-interrupt + unexpected claim** — "I stopped replying to DMs for 2 weeks. 40% of my leads closed themselves."
- **Contrarian against received wisdom** — "Everyone says content compounds. It doesn't — unless you do this."
- **Specific outcome in prospect's language** — "How I booked 14 discovery calls from one Loom."
- **Curiosity gap with payoff** — "The reply that cost me $4,000." (Godin: tell a story worth a tribe.)

### For offer / sales pages ([[MARKETING_CANON]] § Dunford Sales Pitch)
4-part structure, in order:
1. **Insight** — a truth about the market buyer doesn't know yet
2. **Alternatives + their flaws** — what they'd do if we didn't exist
3. **Differentiated approach** — our unique attribute + why it delivers value
4. **Payoff** — what owning this gets them

### For DMs / 1:1 outreach ([[MARKETING_CANON]] § Miner NEPQ)
Neutral tonality. Objection-inviting reframes.
- Situation question → Problem awareness → Consequence → Qualifying → Transition → Offer
- Never "pitch" in first message. Earn the right via curiosity.
- **Anti-pattern:** "Hi, I help [persona] achieve [outcome] with [mechanism]." Delete and try again.

### For paid ads (all platforms)
- **First 3 seconds:** hook. If it doesn't stop the thumb, the rest is dead.
- **Show the outcome, not the product.** Thriving business over handshake stock.
- **Captions always** — 85% watch without sound.
- **9:16 vertical for Stories/Reels** — 40% lower CPC vs 1:1 (benchmark from [[sunbiz-funding-ad-research]]).

## The Value Equation Prompt ([[MARKETING_CANON]] § Hormozi)

Every offer, landing page, and DM sequence answers:

> **Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) / (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)**

| Lever | Copy move |
|-------|-----------|
| Dream Outcome ↑ | Specific transformation in buyer's own words. Not "grow your business" — "book 10 discovery calls in 30 days." |
| Perceived Likelihood ↑ | Social proof with NUMBERS + FACES. Testimonial + before/after metric. |
| Time Delay ↓ | Cohort format, milestone weeks, "first win in 7 days." |
| Effort & Sacrifice ↓ | Templates, done-with-you components, scripts, swipe files. |

## Anti-Slop Guardrails (Immediate Rejection List)

These phrases get your draft killed. No exceptions:

### Banned openers
- "Unlock the power of..."
- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "It's worth noting that..."
- "Let's dive in..."
- "I'm excited to share..."
- "Are you struggling with...?"

### Banned structural tics
- 5-bullet lists where 2 would do
- Generic gradient CTAs / corporate stock imagery
- Em-dash-comma-em-dash — verbal padding — that says nothing
- Emoji strings in B2B copy (🚀✨💯)
- "Game-changer" / "next-level" / "leverage" / "holistic" / "seamless" / "innovative"
- Passive voice ("was built by us" → "we built it")

### Banned structures
- "Not only [X] but also [Y]" — two weak claims stacked
- "That's where we come in" — kills narrative tension
- "This isn't just [X], it's [Y]" — self-important rhetorical flourish
- Paragraph that doesn't earn its existence

**Rule:** If you can delete a sentence and the meaning survives, delete it. ([[MARKETING_CANON]] § Rory Sutherland — every word should be signalling something.)

## Compliance Rails (per-brand)

### SunBiz / MCA (always)
- NEVER: "loan," "lender," "lending," "borrower," "interest rate"
- ALWAYS: "advance," "funding," "capital," "funder," "merchant," "factor rate"
- Meta ads require `special_ad_categories: ['CREDIT']`
- See [[sunbiz-funding]] + [[PATTERNS]] § Meta MCA Ad Category

### OASIS (always)
- Use "Conaugh McKenna" externally. NEVER "CC" in paid ads or B2B prospecting.
- Internal nickname is for internal docs only.
- See [[conaugh-personal]] non-negotiable rules.

### All brands
- No medical / financial / income guarantees without disclaimers.
- No engagement-bait ("comment YES for more" on Meta = demotion).
- No "before/after" body-image claims anywhere.

## The Canon-Citation Template

Every recommendation / brief / campaign plan ends with:

```
Canon applied: [pillar 1], [pillar 2], [pillar 3]
Reasoning: [1-sentence why each was load-bearing in this decision]
```

Example:
```
Canon applied: Dunford positioning, Hormozi Value Equation, Holmes 60/30/10.
Reasoning: Dunford — OASIS vs DIY automation + SDR-agency was the differentiating
frame. Hormozi — stacked bonuses (PULSE repo + Loom + weekly office hour) raise
Perceived Likelihood. Holmes — 60% educational content cadence was chosen to
target the "not thinking" 30% rather than fight for the crowded 3% bucket.
```

If you can't fill both lines, the brief isn't ready.
