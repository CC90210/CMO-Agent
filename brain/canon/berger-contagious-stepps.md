# Jonah Berger — Contagious (STEPPS)

> *"People don't think about how they look when they share. They share things that make them look a certain way."*

Berger's STEPPS framework answers the question: **why does content spread?** Answer: it makes the sharer look good, solves a problem, or triggers a feeling so strong they need to talk about it. Six drivers. Every viral piece of content hits at least 2-3. Most "how to go viral" content from gurus hits 0.

---

## The STEPPS Framework

### S — Social Currency
People share things that make them look interesting, smart, or in-the-know.
- **Apply:** insider frameworks, counter-narratives, early access info, behind-the-scenes data. CC's build-in-public content generates high social currency — people reshare because resharing signals "I follow someone who builds things."
- **Ask:** does sharing this make the sharer look better? If not, it won't spread.

### T — Triggers
Top-of-mind = tip-of-tongue. Frequent environmental cues cause spontaneous brand mention.
- **Apply:** couple your brand/concept to a high-frequency trigger. OASIS could tie itself to "Monday morning DMs" (a weekly trigger for service owners). Nostalgic ties to "Thursday-night gig prep."
- **Ask:** what daily/weekly moment does the buyer experience where our brand could be the pattern-match?

### E — Emotion
High-arousal emotions (awe, anger, excitement, anxiety) drive sharing. Low-arousal emotions (contentment, sadness) don't.
- **Apply:** awe > admiration. Outrage > disagreement. Surprise > "interesting." Don't optimize for "liked" — optimize for "felt."
- **Anti-pattern:** content that makes buyers feel mild positive sentiment. Mild = ignored.

### P — Public
Private behaviors don't spread. Public ones do. Make the behavior visible.
- **Apply:** case studies with specific named customers are more shareable than anonymous testimonials. "Built in public" content is literally the P in STEPPS. OASIS lead magnets should have a "share-worthy output" (the PULSE repo = shareable artifact).
- **Ask:** is there a visible artifact from using our product/content that the buyer can show off?

### P — Practical Value
Useful content spreads. Not because it's interesting — because sharing it reflects well on the sharer (loops back to Social Currency).
- **Apply:** checklists, calculators, specific frameworks, "here's the exact prompt I use." Make the practical value dense and immediately usable.
- **Ask:** could someone save this and use it within 7 days?

### S — Stories
Stories carry ideas across contexts. A story about a concept spreads further than the concept alone.
- **Apply:** case studies framed as narratives. Founder origin stories. Turnaround stories. Specific-customer-arc stories. The concept rides the story.
- **Anti-pattern:** bullet-list content. Facts without narrative die at the edges of conversations.

## The Virality Test (for any content piece)

Score 0-2 on each STEPPS dimension:
- 0 = doesn't apply
- 1 = weakly applies
- 2 = strongly applies

| Driver | Score |
|--------|-------|
| Social Currency | 0-2 |
| Triggers | 0-2 |
| Emotion (high-arousal) | 0-2 |
| Public | 0-2 |
| Practical Value | 0-2 |
| Stories | 0-2 |
| **Total** | /12 |

Below 6 = unlikely to spread. 8+ = candidate for real reach. 10+ = exceptional.

Most "viral tips" content scores 3-5 (shallow Practical Value only) and then blame "the algorithm."

## Berger's Secondary Insights

### Social proof is not monolithic
Similarity matters more than volume. "1,000 people like me" > "1,000,000 people in general."

### Negative word-of-mouth spreads further than positive
But only when it's VIVID and SPECIFIC. Generic complaints are ignored. Specific, visualizable complaints metastasize. Defend against this by responding to specific complaints with specific acknowledgment — never with "sorry for the inconvenience."

### Influencer follows, not influence
Berger's research showed mid-tier creators (10K-100K followers) drive more actual purchase behavior than mega-influencers. Relevance > reach.

## How Maven Applies This

- **Every flagship content piece is STEPPS-scored pre-launch.** Below 6 = rewrite or cut.
- **CC's content strategy deliberately hits S (Social Currency) + P (Public) + S (Stories)** — build-in-public is the whole formula.
- **OASIS lead magnets designed as shareable artifacts** — the PULSE repo scores high on S (social currency) + P (public) + P (practical value).
- **Retarget high-emotion content, not high-like content.** Like-rate is a vanity metric. Comment-rate + share-rate signal emotion.
- **Case studies are stories, not bullet lists.** Specific-person-specific-outcome narratives.
