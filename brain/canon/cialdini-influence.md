# Robert Cialdini — Influence

> *"People will prefer to say yes to those they know and like."*

Cialdini's seven principles of influence are the operating system of human persuasion. Every sales page, landing page, DM sequence, and ad Maven writes leans on at least 2-3 of these — whether deliberately or not. Knowing them = choosing deliberately; not knowing them = stumbling into them.

---

## The 7 Principles

### 1. Reciprocity
People feel obligated to return favors. Give first, then ask.
- **Apply:** Free PULSE repo before sales call. Loom walkthrough before pitch. Valuable tip in the first DM before the CTA. The obligation is real — honor it by making the free thing genuinely valuable, not bait.
- **Anti-pattern:** "Free download" that's 3 paragraphs of generic content + a hard CTA. Low-trust giveaways poison the well.

### 2. Commitment & Consistency
People stay consistent with prior stated positions — especially written/public ones. Tiny yeses lead to bigger yeses.
- **Apply:** Lead magnet → 3-part email nurture → low-ticket tripwire → discovery call. Each step extracts a small commitment that raises likelihood of the next yes.
- **Anti-pattern:** Jumping from "who are you" to "book a call" in one DM. Skips the commitment ladder.

### 3. Social Proof
People look to others' behavior when uncertain. The more similar the "others," the stronger the effect.
- **Apply:** Testimonials from someone in the prospect's exact vertical + size + region outperform generic 5-stars by 10x. "Jordan, HVAC owner, Ontario" beats "500+ customers."
- **Anti-pattern:** "Trusted by 10,000 businesses" without faces/names. Unverifiable = suspect.

### 4. Authority
People defer to legitimate expertise signals. The signals matter more than the substance — costly to fake, cheap to verify.
- **Apply:** Publish specific diagnostic content ("we tested X across 40 accounts and found…"). Specific > general. Track record > credentials. Let authority emerge from what you *show*, not what you *claim*.
- **Anti-pattern:** "As seen in Forbes" on a paid-placement. Half the market knows. Erodes trust.

### 5. Liking
People buy from people they like. Liking is driven by (a) similarity, (b) compliments, (c) cooperation, (d) familiarity.
- **Apply:** CC's personal brand IS the liking engine for OASIS. Every piece of content that makes CC more relatable increases OASIS close rate downstream. This is the measurable case for [[conaugh-personal]] content investment.
- **Anti-pattern:** Corporate-voice DMs from a logo avatar. Zero liking signal.

### 6. Scarcity
Things are more valuable when they seem limited. Losses loom larger than gains (prospect theory).
- **Apply:** Real cohort caps, real enrollment deadlines, real pricing changes. Use when true. Honor the deadline — every fake deadline erodes the next real one.
- **Anti-pattern:** Perpetual "only 3 spots left" that resets every week. Prospects notice. Brand death.

### 7. Unity
The "we are the same" principle. Shared identity, shared in-group. Stronger than liking — people defer to THEIR TRIBE even against their own judgment.
- **Apply:** Identity-first hooks ("For owner-operators of service businesses…"). Tribe language. Naming a shared enemy (e.g., "marketing agencies that sell strategy but never ship"). Maven's Godin canon overlaps here — see [[godin-permission]].
- **Anti-pattern:** Trying to sell to "everyone" signals belonging to no one.

## Pre-Suasion (the second book, often missed)

> *The most effective persuader is not the one with the best argument — it's the one who has primed the audience to be receptive before the argument arrives.*

Pre-suasion is what happens BEFORE the pitch:
- The image on the landing page hero
- The first word of the email subject line
- The question you ask in the DM before your offer
- The content the prospect consumed in the 72 hours before your ad hit

Maven applies pre-suasion by:
- **Hooks that frame the problem** before the solution
- **Content pillars** (educational 60%) that prime trust weeks before the offer
- **Pre-call teaser emails** (Enns) that demonstrate authority before the discovery call
- **Retargeting sequences** that use social proof + liking touches BEFORE the offer hits

---

## How Maven Applies This (checklist)

Every landing page / sales email / DM sequence should deliberately use 3+ principles:

- [ ] Reciprocity — what did we GIVE before asking?
- [ ] Commitment — what smaller yes precedes the ask?
- [ ] Social proof — which specific, similar-to-buyer testimonial is here?
- [ ] Authority — what proof of expertise is shown (not claimed)?
- [ ] Liking — is there a human face / voice / similarity cue?
- [ ] Scarcity — is there a real, honored constraint?
- [ ] Unity — is there a tribe signal ("you" / "we" / named shared enemy)?

Under 3 principles = weak. 5+ principles = landing page territory.

---

## Anti-Patterns This Canon Catches

- Scarcity fakery — "only 3 left" with no mechanism
- Social proof without specificity — "500+ happy customers"
- Authority theater — Forbes badges, fake credentials
- Reciprocity as bait — free thing that's garbage
- Unity language that's actually tribe-exclusion (punching down)
