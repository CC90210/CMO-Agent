# Donald Miller — StoryBrand

> *"The most successful brands invite customers into a story, where the customer is the hero — not the brand."*

Miller's StoryBrand 7-part framework is the canonical messaging clarity tool. It forces every brand story into a narrative structure audiences are wired to understand. The single most useful thing it catches: **brands that position themselves as the hero, when they should position as the guide**. Customer is Luke; brand is Yoda.

If your hero section takes 30+ seconds to explain, StoryBrand is how you fix it.

---

## The 7-Part StoryBrand Framework (SB7)

Every piece of marketing copy should answer these 7 questions:

### 1. A CHARACTER (the customer, not the brand) wants something.
- **Apply:** Start every landing page with what the BUYER wants — not what the brand does. "You want 10 qualified calls a month, without cold outreach eating your weekends." NOT "We're an AI automation agency."

### 2. Has a PROBLEM.
- Three layers:
  - **External problem** (the surface issue) — "leads leak through DMs"
  - **Internal problem** (the feeling) — "I feel like I'm leaving money on the table every day"
  - **Philosophical problem** (the deeper wrong) — "owner-operators shouldn't lose deals to tech they could afford"
- The external problem is what you sell; the internal + philosophical problems are what they BUY.

### 3. Meets a GUIDE (the brand).
- You are NOT the hero. You are the guide. The guide shows empathy + authority.
- **Empathy:** "We know what it's like to refresh DMs at 11pm." (Signals you understand.)
- **Authority:** "We've built 40+ of these for service businesses like yours." (Signals capability — see [[cialdini-influence]] § Authority.)

### 4. Who gives them a PLAN.
- Reduce cognitive load. Walk buyers through 3-5 clear steps.
- **Apply:** "1. Book a 30-min call. 2. We map your DM flow. 3. We ship PULSE in 7 days." Clear plan = reduced risk = higher conversion.

### 5. And calls them to ACTION.
- Two CTAs per page:
  - **Direct CTA** — "Book a call" (the thing you actually want)
  - **Transitional CTA** — "Read the free PULSE guide" (for those not ready; warms them)
- Apply: every page has both. Many pages fail by having only the direct CTA; warm visitors bounce.

### 6. That helps them avoid FAILURE.
- Name what they're losing by NOT buying. Loss aversion (see [[kahneman-system-1-2]]).
- "Every week without this = another 8-12 leads into the void."

### 7. And ends in SUCCESS.
- Paint the after-state concretely. Not just outcomes — identity transformation.
- "You'll be the operator who books 10 calls/month without touching your phone after 6pm."

## The StoryBrand Anti-Patterns

### The "All About Us" Page
Your about page should be about the CUSTOMER. Who they become, not who you are. The brand's origin story goes in a small sidebar — it's not the hero.

### The Brand-as-Hero Trap
Copy that positions the brand as "the revolutionary solution." Customer feels alienated — there's no role for them in the story. Flip it: customer is the hero, brand is the guide.

### The Vague Benefit Section
"Grow your business" — what does that mean? "Book 10 discovery calls this month" — concrete. SB7 forces concrete.

### No Direct CTA
Many B2B sites have "Learn More" as the primary button. That's not a CTA; that's a delay. Direct CTA is always "Book a call" / "Start free trial" / "Buy now."

## The StoryBrand One-Liner

A forced exercise: state your brand in ONE sentence, using the format:

> "We help [hero] solve [external problem] so they can [success state]."

Examples:
- **OASIS**: "We help owner-operators of service businesses automate their DMs so they can close 40% more leads without touching their phone."
- **PropFlow**: "We help small property managers manage tenants + screening so they can stop patching Excel at 11pm."
- **CC Personal**: "We help CC build in public so he becomes the go-to operator-voice for solo founders."

If you can't state it in one sentence = message isn't clear. Rework before writing anything downstream.

## How Maven Applies This

- **Every new landing page template runs SB7 audit before shipping.** Miss any step = iterate.
- **Every brand has a documented StoryBrand one-liner** in its `brain/clients/<brand>.md` file. (TODO: audit current client files and add.)
- **Direct CTA + Transitional CTA on every page.** Transitional CTA doubles the capture rate on cold traffic.
- **Test empathy + authority on same page.** Empathy-only = you understand but aren't capable. Authority-only = you're capable but don't care. Both = you're the guide.
