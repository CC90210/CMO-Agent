# Drew Eric Whitman — Cashvertising

> *"Your prospects don't buy your products. They buy what your products DO FOR THEM."*

Whitman's *Cashvertising* distills 40+ years of direct-response psychology into the **LF8 (Life Force 8)** — the eight biological drives that power all consumer behavior — plus a secondary "Learned 9" of socially-conditioned drives. Practical, ruthlessly tactical. The copy equivalent of a field medic's kit.

Reads like plumbing; performs like cardiology. Every top direct-response copywriter under 40 owns a dog-eared copy.

---

## The LF8 — Life Force 8 (biological drives)

Humans are wired to pursue these eight things. Every piece of high-converting copy hooks into at least ONE — often three or four stacked.

1. **Survival, enjoyment of life, life extension**
   - Fear of death, pain, disease. Health supplements, safety products, wellness SaaS.
   - Copy cue: "stop this [painful outcome] before it costs you [bigger painful outcome]."

2. **Enjoyment of food and beverages**
   - Pleasure signals. Food, dining, premium consumables.
   - Copy cue: sensory language, indulgence framing.

3. **Freedom from fear, pain, danger**
   - Safety, security, peace of mind. Insurance, protection, backup.
   - Copy cue: "sleep at night knowing…"

4. **Sexual companionship**
   - Attraction, partnership, intimacy. Dating, grooming, fashion-as-attraction.
   - Copy cue: transformation framing ("people will notice").

5. **Comfortable living conditions**
   - Ease, convenience, status of surroundings. Home, furniture, smart home.
   - Copy cue: "effortless" framing, visual ease.

6. **To be superior, win, keep up with the Joneses**
   - Status, social comparison, achievement. Luxury, premium upgrades, recognizable badges.
   - Copy cue: "join the 3% who…" Status signals.

7. **Care and protection of loved ones**
   - Family-protection drive. Strongest driver of B2C spending for parents.
   - Copy cue: "for those who depend on you."

8. **Social approval, belonging**
   - Tribe acceptance, identity signals. Community products, branded apparel, group memberships.
   - Copy cue: "for [tribe]…", unity (see [[cialdini-influence]] § Unity).

---

## The Learned 9 — Secondary drives (socially conditioned)

Not hardwired, but reliably activated in modern Western markets:

1. To be informed
2. Curiosity
3. Cleanliness
4. Efficiency
5. Convenience
6. Dependability / quality
7. Expression of beauty / style
8. Economy / profit (for B2B: ROI)
9. Bargains (the SAVINGS frame vs. the COST frame)

**B2B rule of thumb:** LF8 Drive #6 (superiority / winning) + Learned Drive #8 (economy / ROI) + Learned Drive #4 (efficiency) are the load-bearing B2B drives. Stack all three = high-conversion B2B copy.

## Whitman's 17 "Psychological Devices" (applied to copy)

Whitman documents 17 persuasion mechanisms — many overlap with Cialdini. Non-overlapping highlights:

### Fear Factor
Light fear sells; heavy fear paralyzes. Threshold: raise the stakes enough to trigger action, not so much that the reader's brain shuts down.

### Inoculation Theory
Pre-empt objections *before* the buyer voices them. "You might be wondering if this works for [segment] — here's why it does…" Pre-answered objections close faster than rebutted ones.

### Means-End Chain
Don't stop at the feature. Chain: Feature → Benefit → Emotional Payoff → Identity. "Auto-DM reply (feature) → books calls while you sleep (benefit) → never miss a lead (emotional) → you're the operator who's always on (identity)."

### Comparison Pricing
$3,000 looks cheap next to $10,000. Anchor high, then present the real offer.

### Quantified Specifics
"47% improvement" > "significant improvement." Numbers bypass System 2 skepticism (see [[kahneman-system-1-2]]).

### Show the Cost of NOT Buying
"Every month without this = $X lost." Loss aversion applied to opportunity cost.

### Emotional Fueling
Use words that trigger emotion, not just describe fact. "Crush" vs. "improve." "Protect" vs. "secure." Verbs matter.

## The Cashvertising Headline Test

Whitman's rule for headlines: **does it stop the reader because it hits at least one LF8 drive + promises a specific outcome?**

Weak: "Boost your marketing with AI" — hits Learned #8 (ROI) weakly, no specific outcome.
Strong: "How I booked 14 calls last month without ever answering my DMs" — hits LF8 #5 (comfort/ease) + #6 (superiority/winning) + specific outcome.

Every headline Maven writes passes this test or iterates.

---

## How Maven Applies This

- **Every sales page copy mapped to 3+ LF8 drives explicitly.** Annotated in the draft: "This paragraph hits LF8 #3 + #6."
- **Means-End Chain on every benefit.** Feature → Benefit → Emotional → Identity. Don't stop at the feature. Don't stop at the benefit.
- **Inoculation Theory before objection handlers.** Every sales page pre-empts the top 3 objections in the copy flow, not in a separate FAQ.
- **Show cost of NOT buying on every page.** Whitman's loss-aversion plug. One paragraph minimum.
- **Quantified specifics over adjectives.** Replace "many," "fast," "better" with numbers.
