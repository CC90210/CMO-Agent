# Agency — Pricing Playbook

> For consultancies, marketing agencies, dev shops, fractional execs. Applies directly to OASIS AI.

**Canonical voices**: Blair Enns (*Pricing Creativity*, *Win Without Pitching Manifesto*), David C. Baker (*The Business of Expertise*), Chris Do (The Futur — value-based pricing), Alex Hormozi (Value Equation), Rory Sutherland (signalling > functionality).

---

## The 4 pricing models (Enns's hierarchy)

Listed from WORST to BEST for agency economics:

### 1. Hourly billing (Enns: "the tyranny of the time sheet")
- **Why it's bad**: caps margin at your labor cost, punishes speed, rewards incompetence, makes you a commodity
- **Use only when**: ad-hoc work from an existing trusted client, <$500/mo total
- **Rate guidance (for reference only, not a target)**: $150-$500/hr depending on specialty

### 2. Fixed-fee project
- **Why it's better than hourly**: client pays for the outcome, not the effort. Finish fast = higher margin.
- **Risk**: scope creep. Always attach a scope statement + change-order clause.
- **Anchor**: price at 3-5x your estimated cost. Factor in revision cycles.

### 3. Retainer (recurring monthly)
- **Why it's the agency gold standard**: predictable MRR, deeper client relationship, compounds over time
- **Anchor**: $2K/mo minimum for OASIS. $5K/mo is the sweet spot for automation-agency retainers at sub-$500K revenue businesses. $10K-$25K/mo for enterprise.
- **Structure**: scope hours OR scope outcomes. Enns prefers outcomes. Include "strategy calls included" + "X hours of build" + "priority response".

### 4. Value-based / outcome-based (Enns + Do gold standard)
- **Why it's the top**: client pays a % of the value created. 10-20% of tracked revenue from the system.
- **When to use**: only when (a) value is measurable, (b) attribution is clean, (c) you have enough trust to propose it
- **Example for OASIS**: PULSE automation that closes $100K of new business in year 1 → $10K-$20K fee. Cap + floor both apply.
- **Anti-pattern**: proposing value-pricing to a buyer who hasn't bought from you before. They'll reject on principle. Earn it.

## The Three-Tier Anchor (every proposal)

Enns + Chris Do: always present 3 tiers. Middle tier wins ~60% of the time.

Example OASIS retainer:
| Tier | Name | Price | Includes |
|------|------|-------|----------|
| Starter | "Foundation" | $2,000/mo | 1 automation built + maintained, monthly strategy call, email support |
| Pro ⭐ | "Growth Engine" | $5,000/mo | 3 automations built + maintained, bi-weekly strategy, priority support, quarterly planning |
| Custom | "Strategic Partner" | $15,000+/mo | Full automation buildout, weekly strategy, dedicated Slack, CFO/CMO-level advisory |

Anti-patterns:
- Leading with price. Always lead with positioning + case study, then present tiers.
- More than 3 tiers. Decision paralysis. 3 always.
- The middle tier being a weak choice. The middle tier must be the one you WANT them to pick — 60% of buyers will.

## The Hormozi Value Equation (applied to agencies)

**Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) / (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)**

For agency proposals, maximize numerator + minimize denominator:

| Lever | Tactic |
|-------|--------|
| Dream outcome | State in client's own words (from discovery call). "An extra 40 booked sales calls/month by July." |
| Perceived likelihood | Case studies with named clients, before/after numbers, video testimonials |
| Time delay | Deliver first win in week 1. Always. Even if the full build takes 90 days. |
| Effort & sacrifice | Done-for-you everywhere possible. "You won't touch anything until we hand you the dashboard." |

## Discovery-call pricing conversation (scripted)

Enns's move: never quote on the first call. Always:

1. **Qualify the problem** (NEPQ questions, per `skills/verticals/agency/objection-handlers.md`)
2. **Quote a range verbally**: "Projects like this typically run $5K-$15K depending on scope. Is that in the range you were expecting?"
3. **Written proposal** within 24h with 3 tiers
4. **Follow-up call** to walk through proposal — never email the proposal naked

---

## Red lines (do not cross)

1. **Never quote below your walk-away number.** For OASIS: $2K/mo retainer minimum, $5K/project minimum. Declining is always cheaper than resentment-delivering.
2. **Never discount on price; trade on scope.** "I can do it for $3K" → "I can do a reduced scope — PULSE install without the Skool integration — for $3K." Preserve the $5K per-unit price.
3. **Never take equity unless the business is already funded + profitable.** Equity for agency work = 80% of the time, you build, they fail, you made $0.
4. **Never bill hourly for strategy.** Strategy is value, not time. Package as a "strategy sprint" flat fee ($2K-$5K for 2 weeks) or include in retainer.
5. **Never scope-creep for free.** Change order → new price. Polite + consistent.

## Payment terms

- **Retainers**: month in advance, auto-charge (Stripe). No Net-30 for sub-$50K MRR agencies — cash flow matters.
- **Projects**: 50% upfront, 50% on delivery. Or 3x33% (upfront/midpoint/delivery) for longer builds.
- **Enterprise (>$10K/mo)**: quarterly billing with 10% discount acceptable.

---

## Pricing review cadence

- Quarterly: review all active retainers. Any client below current rate card? Raise at renewal.
- Annual: overall rate-card increase 10-20%. Existing clients grandfathered for 12 months, then phased up.
- Per-proposal: quote at current rate card, not "what they'll accept."

---

## Signalling layer (Sutherland)

Prices are signals. A $497/mo "automation package" signals commodity. $5,000/mo signals custom-built-for-you.

For OASIS's B2B positioning, round numbers > psychological pricing. $5,000 > $4,997. The buyer isn't price-optimizing; they're quality-signalling with their internal stakeholders.
