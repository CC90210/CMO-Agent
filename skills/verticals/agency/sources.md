# Agency — Canonical Sources

> The reading, listening, and research pack every agency operator should internalize. Maven cites these when making agency-vertical recommendations.

---

## Tier 1: Must-read (the foundation)

### Blair Enns
- **Why he's canon**: defined modern agency positioning + pricing for the last 20 years. Everything downstream builds on him.
- **Books**: *The Win Without Pitching Manifesto* (2010), *Pricing Creativity* (2018), *The Four Conversations* (2023)
- **Podcast**: 2Bobs with David C. Baker
- **Key ideas Maven applies**:
  - "The pitch" is a race to the bottom; replace it with "the four conversations" (Probative, Qualifying, Value, Closing)
  - Pricing creativity: options > single price; value > hours; anchors > negotiations
  - Expertise commands premium; generalists compete on price
- **Website**: winwithoutpitching.com

### David C. Baker
- **Why he's canon**: the #1 voice on expert positioning, agency sale/acquisition economics, and how to run a profitable creative firm
- **Book**: *The Business of Expertise* (2017), *Secret Tradecraft of Elite Advisors* (2022)
- **Podcast**: 2Bobs (co-hosted with Enns)
- **Key ideas**:
  - "How deep can you afford to be?" — specialization is an economic choice with multipliers
  - Agency positioning triangle: demographic / psychographic / horizontal vs. vertical
  - 40% net margin is the top-quartile benchmark for creative firms

### Chris Do (The Futur)
- **Why he's canon**: made value-based pricing accessible to a generation of design + creative founders via YouTube
- **YouTube**: The Futur (2M+ subscribers), Chris Do channel
- **Books**: *Pocket Full of Do* (2020)
- **Key ideas**:
  - The pricing conversation is a 3-step: frame → discover → close
  - "Burn the boats" — commit to the niche or you'll stay generalist-poor
  - How to have the "what's your budget" conversation without being transactional
- **Website**: thefutur.com

## Tier 2: High-value working reference

### Alex Hormozi
- **Applicable to agencies**: the Value Equation + the 4 lead flows scale to any service business
- **Books**: *$100M Offers* (2021), *$100M Leads* (2023)
- **Key ideas for agencies**:
  - Grand Slam Offer framework (stack bonuses, remove risk)
  - Lead flow diversification — warm / content / paid / cold, never rely on one
  - "Service business" pricing math: 30% is revenue, 30% is delivery, 30% is pipeline, 10% profit floor

### Aaron Ross
- **Book**: *Predictable Revenue* (2011), *From Impossible to Inevitable* (2016)
- **Applicable**: SDR-AE separation when your agency scales past founder-led sales. Cold outbound that doesn't feel spammy.

### Chet Holmes
- **Book**: *The Ultimate Sales Machine* (2007)
- **Applicable**: Dream 100 (identify tier-1 clients + stack touches over 12+ months). Buyer pyramid (most content goes for 3% actively shopping — the win is content for the 60% "not thinking").

### Jeremy Miner
- **Source**: 7th Level, YouTube, NEPQ book
- **Applicable**: every agency sales call. Objection handling without defensiveness.

## Tier 4: Adjacent / supplementary

- **Patrick Lencioni** — *The Advantage*, *Five Dysfunctions of a Team*. For agencies building a team.
- **Ben Horowitz** — *The Hard Thing About Hard Things*. Founder-led growing-pains realism.
- **Daniel Priestley** — *Key Person of Influence*. 5 P's framework for building agency founder authority.
- **Jake Knapp** — *Sprint*. For agencies pitching process to clients.
- **Paul Jarvis** — *Company of One*. For agency founders resisting the "scale for scale's sake" trap.

## Reading order for a new agency founder

1. *Win Without Pitching Manifesto* (Enns) — 90 min, sets the frame
2. *The Business of Expertise* (Baker) — positioning foundations
3. *$100M Offers* (Hormozi) — offer stacking + value equation
4. *Pricing Creativity* (Enns) — operational pricing
5. *Predictable Revenue* (Ross) — when ready to scale past founder sales
6. *The Ultimate Sales Machine* (Holmes) — long-game business development

Total: ~6 books, ~40 hours of reading. Covers 80% of what matters.
