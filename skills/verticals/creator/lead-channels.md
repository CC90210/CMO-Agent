# Creator — Lead Channels + Audience Velocity

> For personal brands, newsletter operators, podcasters, YouTubers, solopreneurs. Applies directly to Conaugh McKenna personal brand + CC's DJ content (Nostalgic Requests roots).

**Canonical voices**: Justin Welsh (*The Diary of a CEO* playbook, solopreneur system), Dan Koe (The One-Person Business), Nicolas Cole + Dickie Bush (Ship 30 for 30, atomic essays), Joe Pulizzi (Content Marketing Institute), Seth Godin (Tribes + Permission Marketing), Tiago Forte (PARA / Building a Second Brain).

## The creator lead-gen stack (in dependency order)

You can't build paid-ad conversion if you have no authority signal. You can't build authority signal if you have no content output. You can't output content without a system. The order matters.

### Layer 0 — The One-Person Content System (Koe + Welsh)

Before any lead gen: can you publish 1 piece of long-form + 1 piece of short-form per day, sustainably, for 90 days?

- **Long-form platform (pick 1)**: LinkedIn article, Substack newsletter, Twitter/X thread, YouTube video
- **Short-form distribution (2-3 max)**: LinkedIn post, Twitter reply, Instagram Reel, TikTok
- **Atomic essay habit** (Cole + Bush): one idea = one post. Not 15 ideas jammed into one.
- **PARA + Capture** (Forte): every conversation, DM, podcast clip → notes → source material

If you can't hit 90 days of consistency, no lead-gen tactic below will work.

### Layer 1 — Cross-platform content (the lead-gen engine itself)

Creator leads come from CONTENT PROOF, not ads. The funnel is:

```
Content (free, valuable) → follower → email subscriber → buyer
```

**Welsh's formula** (the 250k LinkedIn → $5M ARR Justin Welsh path):
- 2 long-form posts per week on 1 primary platform
- Repurpose each into 5-7 short-form posts across 2 other platforms
- Every post CTA: "subscribe to my newsletter" — email is the only channel you own
- Weekly newsletter: 1 essay format, same structure every week, 500-1200 words

### Layer 2 — Email list (the only first-party asset)

All social platforms are rented land. Email is owned.

- **Newsletter cadence**: weekly, same day/time. Tuesday 9am ET is the dominant slot.
- **Welcome sequence**: 3-5 emails over first 7 days. Introduce yourself, deliver lead magnet, soft-pitch flagship offer.
- **Segmentation**: start with 1 list. Only segment when you have >5,000 subs.
- **Target growth**: 100 subs/mo in month 1-3, 500 subs/mo by month 6, 2,000/mo by month 12 (if consistent).

### Layer 3 — Lead magnet → paid offer

Creators monetize through 1 of 4 tiers (Welsh's model):
1. **Free**: newsletter, content
2. **Low-ticket** ($25-$99): digital product (ebook, templates, guide)
3. **Mid-ticket** ($200-$1,000): course, cohort
4. **High-ticket** ($2,500+): 1:1 coaching, consulting, done-with-you programs

Typical mix at $10K/mo revenue: 70% mid-ticket + 20% high-ticket + 10% low-ticket. Reverse common wisdom: high-ticket is easier to sell 10 of than low-ticket is to sell 1,000 of.

### Layer 4 — Collab / cross-pollination (creator-specific)

- **Podcast guesting**: 20-50 episodes in year 1. Not your own — appear on others' with their audience.
- **Newsletter swaps**: guest-write for 2-3 adjacent newsletters per quarter. Grows list 10x faster than paid.
- **Collabs**: 1 collab/month with a creator ~1 tier larger. Their audience gets a taste, your audience gets a boost.
- **"In good company" principle** (Welsh): always be in public with creators you want to be seen with.

### Layer 5 — Paid (LAST, and only when content is producing inbound)

Creator-specific paid plays:
- **Meta retargeting** of site + email opt-in page visitors (cheap, closes the loop)
- **Twitter/X ads on content that already hit** (amplify winners, don't manufacture them)
- **Newsletter sponsorships in adjacent newsletters** (higher ROI than platform ads for B2B-adjacent creators)
- **Never paid ads to cold audiences in year 1.** You haven't earned it yet.

## Platform-specific hook inventory (Nicolas Cole-style)

### Twitter/X
- **Opinion hook**: "Unpopular opinion:" / "Most [audience] don't want to hear this:"
- **List hook**: "7 lessons from [credibility]" / "5 things no one tells you about [niche]"
- **Story hook**: "4 years ago I [low point]. Today I [high point]. Here's how:"
- **Callout hook**: "[Common advice] is bad advice. Here's what actually works:"

### LinkedIn
- **Authority transfer**: "I work with Fortune 500 companies and here's what I've learned"
- **Contrarian take**: "Everyone says [X]. After [N years] I can tell you [Y]."
- **Before/after narrative**: personal transformation stories, always with specific numbers
- **Curated lesson**: "100+ conversations with [ICP] taught me 3 things"

### Instagram Reels / TikTok (short video)
- **First 1 second**: visual pattern interrupt + text overlay with the payoff
- **Hook formulas**: "POV: you just realized...", "If you're [audience], stop doing X", "[Surprising stat] and here's why it matters"
- **30-60s duration**: sweet spot for both platforms. Over 90s kills completion.

### YouTube long-form
- **Title formula**: [Desired outcome] in [timeframe] / [N lessons from X experience]
- **Thumbnail**: face + text <6 words + color contrast
- **First 15 seconds**: preview the payoff, then deliver in full

## Audience velocity metrics (what to actually track)

Creators burn cycles tracking vanity. Track these 5 only:

| Metric | Formula | Target (year 1) |
|--------|---------|-----------------|
| Email subs / post | (new subs this week) / (posts published this week) | >2 |
| Cost per sub (if paid) | spend / new subs | <$3 |
| Email → buyer rate | buyers / subs | 1-3% |
| Revenue per sub | revenue / subs | $2-$10/sub-year |
| Content → inbound DMs / week | count | 5-20 |

Kill metric: follower count, per se. 10k followers who don't buy << 500 subs who do.

## Anti-patterns

1. **Posting on 5+ platforms**: dilutes output + quality. 1 primary + 2 distribution max.
2. **Chasing viral moments**: Sharp's mental-availability research says consistency beats spikes. One viral post + 3 months of silence = lost momentum.
3. **Switching niches every 3 months**: confuses audience, breaks compounding. Commit to a niche for 12 months minimum.
4. **Ignoring email while growing social**: when the algorithm changes, your social audience disappears. Email does not.
5. **Creating offers before audience**: you can sell $99 to 1,000 subs. You can't sell $2,500 to 10,000 followers. Audience without offer = $0.
