# Creator — Objection Handlers

> Objections specific to creator offers (courses, cohorts, coaching, paid newsletters). NEPQ-structured. Use in sales conversations, DMs, comment replies, objection-pre-empt copy on sales pages.

**Canon**: Jeremy Miner (NEPQ), Justin Welsh (solopreneur sales), Alex Hormozi (Value Equation objections), Seth Godin (permission + trust).

---

## The 10 most common creator-buyer objections

### 1. "I can get this info for free on YouTube / Google / Reddit"
- **Decode**: true AND false. True: raw info is free. False: curation, sequencing, accountability, community, and feedback are NOT free.
- **NEPQ response**: "Totally — there's a lot out there. What's stopped you from putting it together on your own so far?"
- **Listen for**: time, overwhelm, starting/stopping, no accountability. These are the real pains. Sell to them.
- **Reframe**: "The content isn't what you're paying for. The structure, the accountability, and the fact that it gets you from A to B in 6 weeks instead of 2 years — that's the actual product."
- **Proof**: cohort testimonials where buyer specifically mentions "I tried to self-teach for 2 years and got nowhere."

### 2. "I don't have time"
- **Decode**: priority mismatch. The pain of not-buying isn't sharp enough.
- **NEPQ response**: "Fair. When you think about where you want to be in 12 months on [topic], is it going to be any easier to find time then?"
- **Hormozi move**: reduce effort + time-delay inputs on the Value Equation. "The cohort is 2 hours/week. Most students report saving 5+ hours/week after week 3 by applying the systems. So it's a net time-giver, not a time-taker."
- **Offer the smaller commitment**: mini-course, waitlist for next cohort, free 5-email series as proof-of-concept.

### 3. "I need to wait until I have more [subs / followers / revenue / experience]"
- **Decode**: false anchor. They're letting "not enough yet" be permanent.
- **NEPQ response**: "What would 'enough' look like? And what's the plan for getting there?"
- **Their answer often reveals no plan.** That's the opening.
- **Reframe**: "This program IS the plan for getting there. Waiting until you have X is like waiting until you're not cold to start the fire."

### 4. "Is this really worth $[price]?"
- **Decode**: perceived-likelihood is weak. They believe the outcome, not the probability.
- **NEPQ response**: "Worth it compared to what?"
- **Listen for comparison point**: free YouTube, another course, doing it themselves, paying a coach. Once you know the alternative, you can position against it.
- **Hormozi move**: stack the Value Equation specifics. "The dream outcome is [X]. The perceived likelihood is [specific social proof]. The time to first result is [Y weeks]. The effort required is [Z hrs/week]. Divide the price over the next 3-5 years of compounding benefit."
- **Never defend with emotion.** Defend with math.

### 5. "I've bought courses before that didn't work"
- **Decode**: trust wound. Don't dismiss it.
- **NEPQ response**: "That's frustrating. What made those not work for you?"
- **Listen for**: no accountability, no community, no feedback, poor sequencing, "guru" format.
- **Address specifically**: "In this cohort, the structure is different in [X/Y/Z] specific ways." Show don't tell.
- **Offer the pilot / guarantee**: 7-day full-refund window. Lowers risk, increases close rate.

### 6. "I'm not sure I'm your ideal customer"
- **Green flag**: they're self-filtering, which means they've engaged seriously with the page.
- **NEPQ response**: "What makes you unsure?"
- **Listen for**: scale mismatch (they're too small/big), domain mismatch (they're in an adjacent niche), experience mismatch (too beginner/advanced).
- **Honest answer wins**: if genuinely not a fit, say so. "You're right — at your stage, this would be overkill. Here's what I'd do in your shoes instead: [free resource]." The goodwill is worth more than the $2,500 sale. (And they'll be your best referrer.)
- **If it IS a fit**: "I hear that a lot. Here's why you fit: [3 specific reasons from their profile]."

### 7. "Can I get a payment plan?"
- **Good sign**: friction is price, not value.
- **Default response**: offer 2-3 payments instead of one. Total slightly higher ($2,500 one-time OR $950 × 3 = $2,850).
- **Risk**: payment-plan defaults. Use Stripe / Kajabi recurring — auto-charge, clear ToS.
- **Never offer a 12-month plan.** Payment plans should close in <90 days total.

### 8. "Do you have testimonials from people like me?"
- **Decode**: perceived-likelihood + social proof gap.
- **Best response**: 3-5 video testimonials from narrow-ICP buyers, each tagged with what the buyer came in with ("I was 0 subs, now 5,000 in 6 months — niche: B2B SaaS").
- **If you don't have ICP-specific proof**: be honest. "Most of my cohort has been [X profile]. You're closer to [Y profile]. I don't have a direct testimonial match, so here's what I can offer: [smaller pilot / refund guarantee / call with past buyer]."

### 9. "When's the next cohort?"
- **Decode**: missed the current window. Real interest.
- **Response**: "Next cohort opens [date]. Here's the waitlist link." Waitlist = email + reason for interest = primed for next launch.
- **Never extend a closed cohort for one buyer.** Breaks the deadline integrity for the next 10 launches.

### 10. "I don't really DO courses — I learn by doing"
- **Decode**: they're identifying as an autodidact. Don't argue with identity.
- **Reframe**: "Makes sense. So do I — this isn't a sit-and-watch course. Week 1 you publish a real thing. Week 2 you pitch a real person. Week 3 you get feedback on real work. The 'course' part is 20% video, 80% doing."
- **Ask**: "When you say you learn by doing — have you done [specific outcome this course promises]? What got in the way?"
- **The answer is usually**: no accountability, no feedback loop. Which the cohort provides.

## Sales-page objection pre-empt pattern

Put these on the sales page (FAQ section, testimonial captions, or inline in copy):

```
"I was skeptical because I'd bought courses that didn't work. What was different here: [specific structural difference]."
— [Name, specific outcome]
```

```
"I thought I didn't have time — turns out the 2 hours/week saved me 10 hours/week once I applied week 2's system."
— [Name]
```

Pre-empting > handling live.

## What Maven should do when drafting creator offer copy

1. List the top 5 objections the ICP will have (from VoC research — see `skills/marketing-research/SKILL.md`)
2. For each, find a real testimonial that addresses it OR plan a question to ask in sales conversations
3. Embed 3-5 objections into the sales page as "I thought this, then I realized that" format
4. Build a 5-email post-click sequence that handles remaining objections one per email
