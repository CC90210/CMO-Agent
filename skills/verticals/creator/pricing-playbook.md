# Creator — Pricing Playbook

> For personal-brand creators, newsletter operators, course creators, solopreneurs. Applies to Conaugh McKenna personal brand (B2B-positioned) + any creator CC dogfoods for.

**Canonical voices**: Justin Welsh (4-tier monetization), Dan Koe (One-Person Business), Alex Hormozi (Value Equation), Seth Godin (smallest viable audience), Nicolas Cole (product ladder for writers).

---

## The creator 4-tier ladder (Welsh's model)

Creators who hit $10K+/mo revenue tend to converge on this structure:

| Tier | Price range | % of revenue (typical mix) | Purpose |
|------|-------------|----------------------------|---------|
| Free | $0 | — | Top-of-funnel, audience growth |
| Low-ticket | $25-$99 | 10-20% | Entry purchase, buyer identification |
| Mid-ticket | $200-$1,000 | 50-60% | Core engine — cohort courses, digital products |
| High-ticket | $2,500-$25K | 20-40% | 1:1 coaching, consulting, DWY programs |

Notes:
- At $10K/mo, most creators are over-indexed on one tier. Diversify.
- High-ticket scales revenue with minimum audience size. 20 buyers × $5,000 = $100K.
- Low-ticket scales AUDIENCE via gifts / lead magnets more than revenue.

## Pricing psychology for creators (Sutherland + Hormozi)

### Signalling
A creator charging $97 for a course signals: commodity, low-stakes, high-volume play.
A creator charging $2,500 for a cohort signals: expertise, curated, outcome-focused.

Buyers are signalling to THEMSELVES — "I'm serious enough to pay $2,500 for this." That signal is worth more than the price delta itself. Aim higher than comfortable.

### The $7 test (Hormozi)
If your free content is better than a competitor's paid product, you've earned the right to charge premium.
If your paid product isn't 10x better than your free, you haven't.

### Value Equation applied to courses
**Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) / (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)**

| Lever | Creator-specific move |
|-------|----------------------|
| Dream outcome | Promise specific transformation in customer's words ("your first 1,000 newsletter subs", not "become a better writer") |
| Perceived likelihood | Social proof: transformation testimonials with specific numbers + faces |
| Time delay | Cohort format with milestone weeks → faster wins than "self-paced" |
| Effort | Templates + scripts + done-for-you components reduce perceived work |

## The creator offer stack (what to actually sell)

### Free offers (lead magnets)
- Newsletter subscription
- Mini-course (5 emails over 5 days)
- Template / swipe file / checklist
- Community free tier (Skool, Discord)

### Low-ticket ($25-$99)
- Ebook / PDF guide (typical: $29-$49)
- Template pack / Notion template (typical: $49-$99)
- Short video course / masterclass recording (typical: $49-$99)

### Mid-ticket ($200-$1,000)
- Cohort-based course (4-6 weeks, $500-$1,000 typical)
- Self-paced full course ($200-$500 typical)
- Annual paid newsletter tier ($250-$500)
- Community paid tier (Skool / Circle, $50-$100/mo)

### High-ticket ($2,500-$25K)
- 1:1 coaching packages (6-12 sessions, $2,500-$10K)
- Done-with-you consulting ($5K-$25K)
- Mastermind access ($500-$2,000/mo)
- Paid speaking / workshops ($5K-$25K per event)

## Cohort vs. self-paced pricing

**Cohort** = higher price, lower volume, higher completion rate, better testimonials.
**Self-paced** = lower price, higher volume, low completion (~5%), testimonial drought.

Recommendation: START with cohort at $500-$1,000. Self-paced is launched AFTER cohort proves demand. Reverse-engineer self-paced FROM cohort recordings once you've run 3 cohorts.

## The launch pricing move (Brunson + Welsh)

### Founder's pricing (first cohort)
- 50-70% of target list price
- Explicit: "this is the first cohort, you're the pilot, the price reflects that"
- Benefit to buyer: lower risk, founder access, testimonial opportunity
- Benefit to creator: testimonial gold + iteration input + revenue while building

### Waitlist + early-bird
- List open → 48-hour early bird at 15-20% off → full price until close → close = deadline matters
- Scarcity is psychological pricing. Don't fake deadlines. Honor them.

### Launch cadence
- 4 cohorts/year maximum. More = cannibalizes next cohort.
- 6-week launch sequence: 3 weeks audience-building → 2 weeks cart-open → 1 week urgency close.

## Pricing a newsletter (Substack / Beehiiv)

### Free-only (year 0-1)
Focus on list growth. 0 subs → 1,000 subs on free only. Monetize via affiliates / lead magnets to high-ticket service.

### Paid newsletter tiers (year 1+)
- **$5-$10/mo** — if your audience is B2C / consumer
- **$15-$30/mo** — if your audience is prosumer / small-business
- **$50-$100/mo** — if your audience is senior operators / executives (what Lenny charges, what Stratechery charges)
- **Annual discount**: 20-30% off monthly

Convention: free tier 80% of the value, paid tier 20% that's operational + confidential. Exclusive > encyclopedic.

---

## Red lines

1. **Never discount mid-cohort.** Breaks the "founder's pricing was an anchor" integrity.
2. **Never stack 5+ tiers.** Decision paralysis. 3-4 max.
3. **Never quote time-and-materials for coaching.** Package. $2,500 for "6 sessions" > $400/hour × 6.
4. **Never compare price to competitors on the sales page.** You're not that commodity.
5. **Never price-drop to close a wavering buyer.** Lose the sale, keep the pricing integrity. The next 10 buyers benefit.

## The smallest viable audience (Godin, applied)

Creators who try to "serve everyone" end up serving no one. Pick an audience small enough that 1,000 true fans is reachable.

For Conaugh McKenna:
- **Not-audience**: "founders" / "marketers" (too broad)
- **Audience**: owner-operators of $250K-$2M service businesses interested in AI automation, Ontario / US Northeast
- **Check**: can you name 50 specific people who match? If yes, audience is reachable. If no, narrow further.

Pricing follows audience narrowness. Narrower audience = higher willingness to pay = higher prices.

---

## Pricing review cadence

- Every 3 cohorts: raise cohort price by 10-25%
- Every 12 months: full pricing ladder review
- Never raise price without adding value to justify — bonus, faster delivery, better community, new module
