# Creator — Canonical Sources

> Reading + listening pack for creators building personal brands, newsletters, courses, cohorts, solopreneur businesses. Maven cites these when making creator-vertical recommendations.

---

## Tier 1: Must-read (the foundation)

### Justin Welsh
- **Why canon**: took LinkedIn to 500k+ followers + $5M+ solo revenue on a repeatable, systemized content-to-revenue machine. Most copied playbook of the last 5 years.
- **Primary source**: LinkedIn posts (public) + The Diary of a CEO appearance + his own paid courses (Content OS, LinkedIn OS, Operating System)
- **Newsletter**: The Saturday Solopreneur
- **Key ideas Maven applies**:
  - 2 long-form posts/week + 3-5 short repurposed daily
  - 4-tier product ladder (free / $25-99 / $200-1k / $2.5k+)
  - Creator operator system — production calendar + analytics review cadence + systematized engagement
- **Website**: justinwelsh.me

### Dan Koe
- **Why canon**: frames creator economy as "the One-Person Business" — philosophy + practical system for solo operators with leveraged income
- **Books**: *The Art of Focus* (2024)
- **Platforms**: YouTube (Dan Koe) + Twitter + paid community
- **Key ideas**:
  - Niche is the intersection of what you want to learn about × what you want to be known for × what you can get paid for
  - "Build the business around your interest" — creator burnout comes from misaligned niche
  - Digital product > service business (for creators — but services fund the early years)

### Nicolas Cole + Dickie Bush
- **Why canon**: rebuilt the "how to write online" education category. Ship 30 for 30 → Premium Ghostwriting Academy.
- **Books (Cole)**: *The Art & Business of Online Writing* (2020), *The Art & Business of Ghostwriting* (2022)
- **Key ideas Maven applies**:
  - "Atomic essays" — one idea = one piece, not a kitchen-sink collection
  - Iron-triangle of online writing: Point → Progression → Payoff
  - Hook frameworks (the 10 canonical openers)

### Joe Pulizzi
- **Why canon**: founded the Content Marketing Institute. Decades of rigorous study on how content becomes a business.
- **Books**: *Content Inc.* (2015, 2nd ed 2021), *Killing Marketing* (2017)
- **Key ideas**:
  - The Content Inc. model: Sweet Spot → Content Tilt → Base → Harvest → Diversify → Sell/Go Big
  - Content-first business building (audience before product)
  - Every creator is a publisher; think like one

### Tiago Forte
- **Why canon**: the systems layer of creator operations. How to capture + organize ideas into repeatable creative output.
- **Books**: *Building a Second Brain* (2022), *The PARA Method* (2023)
- **Key ideas**:
  - PARA (Projects / Areas / Resources / Archive) — knowledge organization that scales
  - CODE (Capture / Organize / Distill / Express) — creative output pipeline
  - Progressive summarization — your highlights become next year's posts

## Tier 2: High-value working reference

### Seth Godin
- **Books**: *Permission Marketing* (1999), *Tribes* (2008), *This Is Marketing* (2018)
- **Newsletter**: seths.blog (daily — a master class in short-form essays)
- **Applicable to creators**: smallest viable audience, permission-based list building, the tribe model for community-backed creators.

### Daniel Priestley
- **Book**: *Key Person of Influence* (2014)
- **Applicable**: the 5 P's — Pitch, Publish, Product, Profile, Partnership — as a build-order for personal authority. Strongest for founder-CEOs building personal brands adjacent to their company.

### Tim Ferriss
- **Books**: *Tools of Titans* (2016), podcast archive (700+ episodes)
- **Applicable**: interview-as-content method, minimum-viable audience concepts, creator-as-studio model.

### Ali Abdaal
- **Sources**: YouTube (5M+ subs), Feel Good Productivity book (2024)
- **Applicable**: productivity + creator + business triangle. Systemized YouTube-to-product funnel case study.

### Lenny Rachitsky
- **Source**: Lenny's Newsletter ($15-20M/yr reportedly, on Substack)
- **Applicable**: B2B-adjacent paid newsletter as a business model. The gold standard for product/growth-niche newsletter economics.

## Reading order for a new creator

1. *This Is Marketing* (Godin) — 4 hours, sets philosophy
2. *The Art & Business of Online Writing* (Cole) — 6 hours, writing craft
3. *Content Inc.* (Pulizzi) — 8 hours, business model
4. *Building a Second Brain* (Forte) — 6 hours, operations
5. Welsh's LinkedIn archive (free) — 3-4 hours of skimming
6. Koe's YouTube back-catalog (free) — pick 10 core videos, ~5 hours

Total: ~30 hours. Covers the core of online-creator economics + craft.

## What Maven cites from this pack

When making a creator-vertical recommendation:

- "Per Welsh, we shouldn't scatter to 5 platforms. Pick 1 primary + 2 repurposed. LinkedIn primary makes sense for OASIS adjacency."
- "Koe's philosophy says niche narrowness > niche breadth. Current positioning is too broad — let's tighten to 1 specific ICP archetype."
- "Cole's atomic essay framework: one idea per post. The current draft is 3 ideas jammed together — we split into 3 posts."
- "Godin's smallest viable audience test: can we name 50 people who fit? If not, the niche is still too broad."
- "Pulizzi's Content Inc. model puts us at the 'Base' stage — audience growing, no product yet. Hold on product launch until list >1,000."

A recommendation without a canonical citation is a draft opinion, not yet a Maven recommendation.
